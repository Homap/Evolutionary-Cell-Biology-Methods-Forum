# Reading Themes

This reading group is organized around Michael Lynch’s *Evolutionary Cell Biology*, but the broader aim is to connect each chapter to modern methodological approaches.

For each chapter, we ask:

1. What is the main cell biological concept?
2. What methods allow us to measure or manipulate this concept?
3. What evolutionary methods allow us to compare, model, or reconstruct it?
4. What open questions could be explored in future research?

## Methodological structure

Each session includes two methodological themes:

| Method type | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Cell or molecular biology method | Measures, visualizes, perturbs, or models cellular mechanisms |
| Evolutionary or comparative method | Compares traits across species, reconstructs history, or models evolutionary processes |

## How specific papers will be chosen

Specific papers will be selected closer to each session based on:

- relevance to the chapter
- accessibility for a mixed audience
- methodological usefulness
- connection to current research in evolutionary cell biology
